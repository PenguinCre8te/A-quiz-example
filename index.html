Urban Problems Quiz

1. What are the urban problems in Hong Kong?

Housing Problems

Question: What are two main issues contributing to housing problems in Hong Kong?

Shortage of housing and poor living environment.

Question: Explain why there is a shortage of housing in Hong Kong.

There is not enough housing, private housing is expensive, and the supply of public housing is insufficient to meet the needs.

Traffic Congestion

Question: List two common locations where traffic congestion occurs in Hong Kong.

Central Business District (CBD) and old urban areas.

Question: When is traffic congestion most common, and what impact does it have?

Traffic congestion is most common during peak hours, causing inconvenience.

Environmental Pollution

Question: Identify and briefly describe four types of pollution affecting Hong Kong.

Air pollution, land/solid waste pollution, water pollution, and noise pollution.

Question: What are the major sources of air pollution in Hong Kong?

Major sources of pollutants are cars and power plants.

Urban Decay

Question: Define urban decay in the context of Hong Kong's old urban areas.

Urban decay refers to the worsening of urban environments in areas that were developed the earliest, where buildings are old and poorly maintained.

Question: What are two problems associated with urban decay in these areas?

Land use conflict and old, worn-out buildings.

Causes of Urban Problems

Question: How has population increase contributed to urban problems in Hong Kong?

It creates heavy demand for land, housing, transport, and other resources, leading to issues such as traffic congestion and environmental pollution.

Question: Give an example of how poor urban planning has led to specific issues.

Roads in urban areas are narrow due to a failure to predict the growth of population and the number of cars, leading to traffic congestion and land use conflict problems.

Tackling Housing Problems

Question: What is the purpose of building new towns and new development areas?

To increase housing supply and provide well-planned communities with private and public housing, open spaces, and various facilities.

Question: Describe how urban renewal can address housing problems.

Urban renewal projects redevelop, rehabilitate, preserve, and revitalize old areas to provide more housing and improve living standards.

Reducing Traffic Congestion

Question: How does expanding the railway network help alleviate traffic congestion?

It is efficient at carrying many people and is environmentally friendly, forming the backbone of Hong Kong's public transport system.

Question: What strategies are used to control the number of cars on the road?

Raising the first registration tax and annual license fee for private cars, improving bus routes, and offering park-and-ride discounts.

Reducing Pollution Problems

Question: What measures are being taken to reduce air pollution from road traffic and power supply?

Phasing out diesel cars, subsidizing the purchase of electric buses, using less coal, and developing renewable energy sources.

Question: How is Hong Kong addressing land pollution and waste management?

Implementing the waste charging scheme, expanding landfills, and setting up incinerators.

Urban Redevelopment and Rehabilitation

Question: What is the difference between urban redevelopment and building rehabilitation?

Urban redevelopment involves pulling down old buildings and replacing them with modern structures, while building rehabilitation involves maintenance and upgrading facilities to slow down aging.

Question: Provide an example of heritage preservation and revitalization in Hong Kong.

The Central Police Station has been revitalized and transformed into Tai Kwun, mainly used for cultural events.

Understanding Urban Issues

Question: Explain how rapid economic development has led to urban problems.

It has increased the standard of living, leading to more people affording cars and increased consumption, causing traffic congestion and land pollution.

Question: Why is it important to tackle urban problems in Hong Kong?

To improve the living environment, ensure sustainable development, and enhance the quality of life for residents.
